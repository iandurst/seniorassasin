Rules

These rules summarize how PCS Senior Assasin will run. Play smart, safe, and with respect for others.

1) Safety first

  • Use only clearly safe equipment if your ruleset allows (e.g., foam-dart or water devices); never bring anything that could be mistaken as dangerous.
  • Never play on campus or during school events. Respect people’s space and time.
  • Obey local laws at all times.

2) Eligibility & sign up

  • Open to PCS seniors.
  • Provide your real name and a working mobile number to receive gameplay texts.
  • New sign-ups are approved by an administrator before becoming active in the game.

3) Scoring & eliminations

  • Eliminations only count if they are recorded and sent to 309‑648‑1257 (Ian Durst) or shown to him at school.
  • Admins verify eliminations and update the leaderboard.
  • Eliminated players are marked “Out”; eliminators receive +1 elimination.

4) Safe zones & no‑go times

  • School, church, and during or directly after sports practices/games are prohibited.
  • Everything else is generally fair game — but don’t be reckless or trespass. Use common sense and always be respectful.

5) Prize pool & weekly vote

  • Everyone pays a $5 entry fee. Think “Squid Game” style voting: each Friday at 3:00 PM Central, a vote opens for 3 hours. If at least 75% of the remaining players (active & alive) choose to stop, the game ends and those players split the prize pool. Otherwise, the game continues.
  • Each time a player is eliminated, their entry fee is added to the prize pool.
  • The prize pool displayed on the home page comes from the entry fee × number of approved participants, unless an admin override is set.

6) Voting details

  • Voting opens automatically on Fridays at 3:00 PM Central and lasts 3 hours. Eligible players receive a text link to vote “Stop the game” or “Continue”.
  • Votes are per‑player, one time only, and are valid only within the window.
  • If ≥75% vote “Stop”, the game ends; otherwise it continues.

These rules may be refined by the administrators for clarity or safety. When in doubt, ask an admin.
